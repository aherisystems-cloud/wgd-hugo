faqs:
  - question: "What size rug do I need for a living room?"
    answer: "For most living rooms, an 8x10 or 9x12 rug works best. The rule of thumb: all furniture legs on the rug, or at minimum the front legs of your sofa and chairs. Leave 12–18 inches of bare floor between the rug edge and your walls. When in doubt, go bigger — a rug that's too small is the most common decorating mistake."
  - question: "What is the best rug material for bedrooms?"
    answer: "For bedrooms, prioritise softness underfoot. Wool is warm, durable, and naturally stain-resistant. Faux fur and shag rugs offer maximum plushness for a luxury feel. Viscose and silk-blend rugs have a beautiful sheen but are more delicate. Avoid jute and sisal in bedrooms — they feel rough underfoot."
  - question: "How do I stop my rug from slipping on hard floors?"
    answer: "Use a rug pad cut slightly smaller than your rug (about 1 inch smaller on each side). Non-slip rug pads grip both the floor and the rug. They also add cushioning underfoot, protect your floor from scratches, and extend the life of your rug. Never skip a rug pad on hard floors."
  - question: "How do I clean a glam or faux fur rug?"
    answer: "For faux fur rugs: shake outside weekly, vacuum on the lowest suction setting with the beater bar off, and spot clean spills immediately with cold water and mild detergent. Never put faux fur in the washing machine. For wool area rugs: vacuum weekly, rotate every 6 months for even wear, and professional clean every 12–18 months."
  - question: "Can I layer rugs?"
    answer: "Yes — rug layering is a key technique in boho and eclectic interiors. Layer a smaller textured rug over a larger flatweave or natural fibre base rug. The bottom rug should be neutral and flat; the top rug adds colour and texture. Secure both with a non-slip pad underneath and between layers."
  - question: "What rug pile height is best for high-traffic areas?"
    answer: "Low pile (under 0.5 inches) or flatweave rugs are best for high-traffic areas like entryways, hallways, and dining rooms. They're easier to clean, more durable, and chairs and furniture legs don't sink in. Reserve high-pile and shag rugs for low-traffic areas like bedrooms and reading nooks."

A rug does more work in a room than any other single element. It anchors the furniture arrangement, defines zones in open-plan spaces, adds warmth and sound absorption, and sets the tactile tone of the entire room. Get the rug right and everything else falls into place. Get it wrong — wrong size, wrong texture, wrong placement — and even the most expensive furniture arrangement looks incomplete.

For glam interiors, a rug is a signature piece. A faux fur rug beside the bed transforms a bedroom into a luxury suite. A high-pile ivory and gold area rug under a glass coffee table instantly elevates the entire living room. The investment is worth it.

This guide covers everything — the types of rugs available, how to choose the right size for every room, what materials to look for, how to care for your investment, and our curated picks at every price point.
