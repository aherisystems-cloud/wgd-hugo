faqs:
  - question: "What size mirror should I get for above a sofa?"
    answer: "As a general rule, a mirror hung above a sofa should be about two-thirds the width of the sofa. For a standard 84-inch sofa, aim for a mirror 48–60 inches wide. The bottom of the mirror should sit 6–8 inches above the top of the sofa back."
  - question: "How do I hang a heavy mirror safely?"
    answer: "For mirrors over 20 lbs, always anchor into wall studs using a stud finder. Use heavy-duty mirror hanging hardware rated for at least twice the mirror's weight. For drywall without studs, use toggle bolts rated for the weight. Never rely on adhesive strips alone for large or heavy mirrors."
  - question: "What is the most flattering mirror angle for a full-length mirror?"
    answer: "Tilt the mirror slightly backward (5–7 degrees from vertical, top leaning away from you). This angle makes legs appear longer and creates a more flattering overall reflection. A perfectly vertical mirror can make you look shorter and wider than you actually are."
  - question: "Are LED vanity mirrors worth it?"
    answer: "Yes — especially for makeup application. Natural daylight simulation (around 5000K) shows colours accurately and eliminates shadows. Look for mirrors with adjustable colour temperature (warm to cool) and brightness. Hollywood-style LED mirrors with individual bulbs give the most even, shadow-free light."
  - question: "How do I clean a gold or brass mirror frame without damaging the finish?"
    answer: "Use a soft dry cloth for weekly dusting. For deeper cleaning, dampen a microfibre cloth with warm water and a drop of mild dish soap — never spray directly on the frame. Dry immediately. Avoid vinegar, bleach, or abrasive cleaners which strip metallic finishes. For tarnished brass, use a dedicated brass polish applied with a soft cloth."
  - question: "Can mirrors make a small room look bigger?"
    answer: "Yes — strategically placed mirrors are one of the most effective ways to make a small room feel larger. The key is to position the mirror to reflect a window or light source, and to place it on the longest wall. Full-length mirrors in small bedrooms and large wall mirrors in narrow hallways are particularly effective."
  - question: "What is an anti-fog bathroom mirror?"
    answer: "Anti-fog mirrors have a heated pad built into the back that warms the mirror surface to just above the dew point, preventing steam from condensing. They require an electrical connection and are typically wired into the bathroom circuit. Worth the investment for primary bathrooms where you shower daily."

A mirror is the most underrated element in interior design. Placed correctly, it doubles your light, creates the illusion of space, and adds a focal point that no artwork or accessory can match. Placed incorrectly — at the wrong height, wrong size, or reflecting the wrong thing — it disrupts the entire room.

For glam interiors, mirrors serve double duty. A gold sunburst mirror on a bare wall instantly transforms it into a statement. A Hollywood LED vanity mirror turns a basic dressing table into a luxury suite experience. Getting your mirrors right is one of the highest-return investments you can make in any room.

This guide covers everything — the types of mirrors available, how to choose the right size and placement, what styles work in each room, and how to care for what you buy.
