faqs:
  - question: "What size lamp should I choose for a bedroom?"
    answer: "For most bedrooms, bedside table lamps should stand 24–30 inches tall total so the bottom of the shade sits at shoulder height when you're sitting up in bed. The shade itself should be wide enough to cast light over a book without creating harsh shadows — 12 to 16 inches diameter is the sweet spot for most nightstands."
  - question: "Can I use LED bulbs in crystal and glam-style lamps?"
    answer: "Yes, and you should. Look for warm white LEDs at 2700K colour temperature — they complement gold and crystal finishes beautifully and create that luxury glow. Avoid daylight LEDs (5000K+) in decorative lamps as they make gold finishes look greenish. Make sure to use dimmable LEDs if you have a dimmer switch installed."
  - question: "How do I make a room look more glamorous with lighting?"
    answer: "Layer your lighting across three types: ambient (overhead), task (table or floor lamp), and accent (sconces, candles, or LED strips). Use warm 2700K bulbs throughout, choose metallic finishes that bounce light, and install dimmers on every circuit. The combination of warm, layered light is what creates that signature luxury hotel atmosphere."
  - question: "What is the difference between brass and gold lamps?"
    answer: "Brass is a natural metal alloy (copper and zinc) with a warm, slightly muted tone that develops a natural patina over time — giving lamps character and a sense of age. Gold finish typically refers to a plating or paint coating that stays consistently bright and reflective. Both work beautifully in glam interiors. Solid brass is more durable and often more expensive; gold-finished lamps are more consistent in colour."
  - question: "How do I clean a crystal lamp without damaging it?"
    answer: "Turn off and unplug the lamp first. Mix equal parts distilled water and white vinegar in a spray bottle. Spray onto a lint-free microfibre cloth (not directly on the lamp) and wipe each crystal individually. Dry immediately with a clean cloth to prevent water spots. For the base, use a gold or chrome safe polish. Repeat 2–4 times per year. Never use bleach, abrasive cleaners, or paper towels."
  - question: "What does K9 crystal mean?"
    answer: "K9 is a grade of optical-quality glass used in decorative lighting. It has excellent clarity and refracts light into a beautiful spectrum of colours, creating the sparkle associated with luxury chandeliers and glam lamps. It is more affordable than genuine Swarovski crystal but still looks stunning in most home settings."
  - question: "Do I need an electrician to install a pendant light?"
    answer: "If you are replacing an existing ceiling light fixture that already has wiring in place, it is typically a DIY job — you connect the same wires (black to black, white to white, green or copper to ground). If there is no existing wiring and you need a new electrical box installed, then yes, an electrician is required. Always turn off the circuit breaker before starting."
  - question: "What wattage should I use in a glam table lamp?"
    answer: "Check the maximum wattage listed on the socket or shade label — most decorative lamps are rated for 40–60 watts. With LED bulbs, use a 9–10W LED equivalent (about 800 lumens) for a bedside lamp, or a 13–15W LED (about 1100 lumens) for a reading or task lamp. Always use dimmable LEDs if the lamp is on a dimmer."

Lighting is the single most transformative element in any room — more than furniture, paint colour, or accessories. The right lamp doesn't just illuminate a space; it defines the mood, flatters the other pieces in the room, and tells visitors exactly what kind of home this is.

For glam interiors, lighting does even more. Crystal drops catch and scatter light. Polished gold bases reflect warmth back into the room. Velvet shades create a soft halo that makes everything look more luxurious. Getting your lamps right is not optional — it is foundational.
