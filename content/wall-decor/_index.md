faqs:
  - question: "How high should I hang wall art?"
    answer: "The standard rule is to hang art so its centre is at 57–60 inches from the floor — average eye level when standing. This applies whether you're hanging a single piece or a gallery wall (treat the whole arrangement as one piece and centre that at eye level). The exception is art above furniture — leave a 6–8 inch gap between the furniture top and the bottom of the art."
  - question: "How do I plan a gallery wall?"
    answer: "Start by tracing each frame on paper and cutting out the shapes. Tape them on the wall to plan the arrangement before making any holes. Start with the largest piece slightly left of centre, then build outward maintaining consistent spacing (2–3 inches between frames). Mix sizes, orientations, and frame styles for interest, but keep a consistent colour palette in the art itself."
  - question: "What size art should I get for above a sofa?"
    answer: "Art above a sofa should be roughly 2/3 the width of the sofa. For an 84-inch sofa, aim for art that's 56–65 inches wide total (this can be one piece or a group of pieces spanning that width). Hang so the bottom of the art is 6–8 inches above the sofa back."
  - question: "Is it OK to mix frame styles in a gallery wall?"
    answer: "Yes — mixing frame styles adds visual interest and feels more curated and personal than perfectly matched frames. The key is to find one unifying element: the same frame colour (all gold, all black, all natural wood), or the same art style, or the same mat colour. One consistent thread ties the whole arrangement together."
  - question: "How do I hang art on plaster walls without damage?"
    answer: "For lightweight pieces under 5 lbs, adhesive strips (like Command strips) work well on plaster if applied correctly. For heavier pieces, use a proper picture hook with a hardened nail driven at a 45-degree angle into the plaster. Always drill into studs for pieces over 10 lbs. Avoid toggle bolts in old plaster as they can crack it."

Your walls are the largest canvas in your home — and most people leave them underutilised. A bare wall isn't neutral. It's a missed opportunity to add personality, warmth, and visual interest that makes a house feel like a home.

For glam interiors, wall décor is where style gets loud. An oversized abstract canvas in black and gold above a velvet sofa. A sunburst mirror flanked by two gold sconces. A gallery wall of framed botanical prints in matching gold frames. These are the moments that make a room unforgettable.

This guide covers everything — the types of wall décor available, how to hang and arrange art like a designer, what works in each room, and how to create a gallery wall that looks intentional rather than chaotic.
