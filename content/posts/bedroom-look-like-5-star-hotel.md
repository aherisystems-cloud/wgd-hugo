## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I make my bedroom look more luxurious on a budget?**

Start with three things: velvet throw pillows in a jewel tone, a chunky knit throw draped across the foot of the bed, and your curtains re-hung at ceiling height rather than above the window frame. These three changes cost under $150 combined — and the transformation is genuinely shocking. Then add a brass floor lamp for the layered lighting that makes the whole room feel intentional. Those four moves alone will change your bedroom completely.

**What colors make a bedroom look expensive and glam?**

The most reliably luxurious bedroom palette uses cream or warm ivory as the base, champagne gold as the metallic connector, and two accent tones in the pillows and throws — dusty rose and blush for feminine glam, emerald and navy for dramatic luxury. The rule that never changes: stay within four colors maximum, and use texture contrast (velvet against linen, marble against brass) to create visual richness rather than adding more colors.

**What is the best type of rug for a luxury bedroom?**

A large faux fur rug in ivory, champagne, or blush is the top choice for a glam bedroom. It adds immediate tactile and visual luxury that lower-pile rugs cannot replicate. The size rule is non-negotiable: go 8x10 minimum for a queen or king bed so the rug extends at least 18 inches on each side. A too-small rug is one of the most common and most visually damaging mistakes in bedroom design — and one of the easiest to avoid.

**Do I need a chandelier for a luxury bedroom?**

No — but if your budget allows for one statement lighting investment, the chandelier wins. Nothing else creates the same "wow" response when guests walk into your room. If a chandelier is out of reach right now, a brass arc floor lamp and two marble bedside lamps create excellent, layered, hotel-quality lighting without any ceiling installation. Start there and add the chandelier when you are ready.

**How many pillows should a luxury bed have?**

A fully styled luxury bed has four layers: two large Euro shams (26x26) at the back, two standard sleeping pillows in matching pillowcases in the middle, two to four decorative velvet pillows at the front, and one long lumbar pillow laid across the very front. That lumbar pillow is the detail most people skip — and it is exactly what makes a hotel bed look professionally arranged rather than just carefully made.

**What type of curtains make a bedroom feel bigger and taller?**

The single most impactful change is where you hang them — from the ceiling, not above the window frame. Combine that with a fabric in the same tone family as your walls (ivory linen against cream walls is the classic pairing) and panels long enough to pool slightly on the floor. These three moves together create a genuinely dramatic sense of height and space that adds a perceived foot or more to your ceiling — for zero extra cost.

**Is velvet still in style for bedroom decor?**

Velvet is having its strongest moment in interior design in years. On bed frames, pillows, benches, and curtains, it is the dominant texture in luxury bedroom design right now — particularly in glam and boutique hotel aesthetics. The shift that makes it feel current rather than traditional: pairing velvet with linen, marble, and brass rather than heavier period fabrics. That combination is warm, tactile, and genuinely stunning.

**How do I style a bedroom bench at the foot of the bed?**

Three steps: place a neatly folded chunky knit throw draped over one end. Add one or two smaller decorative pillows — the same velvet tone as your bed pillows works beautifully. Leave the rest of the surface clean and visible. The bench should be the same width as your bed or slightly narrower. Too wide and it looks like a mistake. Proportioned correctly, it is the single piece that makes every guest say the room looks like a hotel suite.
