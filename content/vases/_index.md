faqs:
  - question: "How do I style a coffee table?"
    answer: "The classic coffee table formula: one tray as a base, one stack of coffee table books, one organic element (plant, vase with stems, or bowl of stones), and one sculptural object. Keep the total height variation interesting — tall book stack, medium vase, low tray — and leave at least 30% of the surface empty. The empty space is part of the design."
  - question: "What flowers or stems look best in glam vases?"
    answer: "For glam interiors: white or cream peonies, white orchids in a sleek vase, dried pampas grass in a tall gold vase, or eucalyptus branches. Avoid overly colourful mixed arrangements — a single-stem variety in a great vase looks more luxurious. Quality faux stems have become indistinguishable from real at a glance and last indefinitely."
  - question: "How do I create a vignette on a shelf?"
    answer: "A good shelf vignette has three elements: height variation (one tall piece, one medium, one low), texture variation (one smooth, one rough, one reflective), and one organic element (plant, vase with stems, or natural object). Use the rule of odds — three or five objects, never even numbers. Leave space between groupings."
  - question: "Are faux flowers and stems worth using?"
    answer: "Yes — quality faux stems have improved dramatically. High-quality artificial pampas grass, eucalyptus, and orchid stems are nearly indistinguishable from real at normal viewing distances. They last indefinitely, require no maintenance, and are ideal for spaces with low light or for anyone who travels. The key is buying quality — cheap faux florals look cheap. Spend the money on a few great pieces."

The difference between a room that looks designed and one that looks lived-in but unfinished is almost always the accessories. The right vases, decorative objects, and accent pieces are what transform a collection of furniture into a home.

For glam interiors, every surface is an opportunity. A gold tray on the coffee table with three perfectly chosen objects. A tall statement vase in the corner with dried pampas grass. A cluster of crystal bud vases on the dining sideboard. These finishing touches are what guests notice and remember.

This guide covers everything — how to choose and style vases and decorative accents, the rules for creating beautiful vignettes, and how to edit ruthlessly for maximum impact.
