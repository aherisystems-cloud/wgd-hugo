faqs:
  - question: "How high should I hang curtain rods?"
    answer: "Hang the rod as high as possible — ideally 4–6 inches below the ceiling, not just above the window frame. This makes ceilings appear taller and windows appear larger. Also extend the rod 6–12 inches beyond the window frame on each side so curtains stack off the glass when open, maximising light."
  - question: "How long should curtains be?"
    answer: "Floor-length curtains always look more elegant than short ones. For a clean modern look, curtains should just graze the floor. For a luxurious romantic look, allow 3–6 inches of pooling on the floor. Avoid curtains that end at the windowsill or mid-wall — this looks unfinished in most settings."
  - question: "How many curtain panels do I need per window?"
    answer: "For a full, luxurious look, each window needs panels that total 2–2.5 times the window width when fully closed. For a 60-inch window, you need 120–150 inches of fabric total. Two 84-inch wide panels gives 168 inches — perfect. Using panels that are too narrow creates a skimpy, flat look."
  - question: "Are blackout curtains really worth it?"
    answer: "For bedrooms, absolutely yes. Studies consistently show that complete darkness significantly improves sleep quality. Blackout curtains also reduce heat gain in summer and heat loss in winter, potentially lowering energy bills. The best blackout curtains today look indistinguishable from regular curtains — the technology is completely hidden in the lining."

Curtains are the most transformative window treatment you can choose. Floor-to-ceiling panels instantly make ceilings feel taller, rooms feel larger, and interiors feel more finished. The wrong curtains — too short, too thin, too narrow — can undermine even the most carefully designed room.

For glam interiors, curtains are non-negotiable. Velvet panels in deep jewel tones or soft neutrals, hanging from ceiling to floor, are what separate a styled room from an ordinary one. This guide covers everything you need to know.
