faqs:
  - question: "Why does my candle tunnel instead of burning evenly?"
    answer: "Tunnelling happens when a candle isn't burned long enough on its first burn. The wax has a memory — it will only melt as wide as it did on the first burn. Always burn a new candle until the entire top surface is liquid, which typically takes 2–4 hours depending on diameter. Never burn a candle for less than 1 hour or more than 4 hours at a time."
  - question: "How long should a luxury candle last?"
    answer: "A quality 8oz jar candle should provide 45–60 hours of burn time. A 10oz candle 60–80 hours. Burn time varies by wax type (soy burns longer than paraffin), wick size, and fragrance load. To maximise burn time: trim the wick to 6mm before every burn, keep away from drafts, and always burn to the edge on the first use."
  - question: "What is the difference between soy and paraffin wax candles?"
    answer: "Soy wax is plant-based, burns cooler and slower, produces less soot, and is considered more eco-friendly. It holds fragrance well but can look uneven (frosting) as it cures. Paraffin wax burns hotter and brighter, holds colour well, and is less expensive. Most luxury candles use a blend of both to get the best properties of each. Pure beeswax is the premium natural option."
  - question: "How do I choose a home fragrance that suits my space?"
    answer: "Match the fragrance character to the room's purpose. Energising spaces (kitchen, home office): citrus, mint, eucalyptus. Relaxing spaces (bedroom, bathroom): lavender, sandalwood, vanilla, chamomile. Social spaces (living room, dining room): warm woods, amber, light florals, clean linen. Entryway: fresh and welcoming — citrus or light floral. Choose one signature scent per floor of your home for coherence."

Scent is the most powerful and most underestimated element in interior design. A beautifully styled room that smells wrong feels wrong — no matter how perfect the furniture or art. Conversely, a room that smells wonderful creates an immediate sense of welcome, comfort, and luxury before anyone looks at a single object.

For glam interiors, candles and scent objects are décor in their own right. A pair of crystal candle holders with white taper candles on a dining table. A luxury scented jar candle in a gold vessel on the bedside table. A marble reed diffuser in the entryway. These pieces do double duty — they look beautiful and they make your home smell like a five-star hotel.

This guide covers everything — the types of home fragrance available, how to choose scents for different rooms, how to make candles last longer, and how to build a signature home fragrance identity.
